# Reflection

- High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: The High-Energy Pop profile finds songs like "Sunrise City" and "Gym Hero" because those songs are close to the target energy and are not acoustic. Chill Lofi shifts to slow, soft songs like "Library Rain" and "Midnight Coding" because it values low energy and acoustic warmth.

- Deep Intense Rock vs Classical Rager: Deep Intense Rock prefers "Storm Runner" because it matches both rock and intense mood, while Classical Rager still keeps "Symphony Dawn" near the top even though the energy is very different. That makes sense because the model still gives a strong boost to exact genre and mood matches.

- High Energy + Melancholic vs Non-Acoustic Lofi: The melancholic profile is a strange mix, so it ends up with a blues song first and then high-energy rock songs after that. Non-Acoustic Lofi still prefers lofi songs despite the acoustic penalty, showing that genre/mood can beat the dislike of acoustic sound.

- Why "Gym Hero" shows up for Happy Pop: Even though "Gym Hero" is intense rather than happy, it is high energy and very non-acoustic, so it scores well for the High-Energy Pop listener. This shows the model is testing for energy and acoustic preference as much as it is testing for mood.
